Clinical Research

Inability to Belch and Associated Symptoms Due to RCPD: Diagnosis and Treatment (2019)

In a study of 51 patients who had long suffered without a clear diagnosis, a targeted botulinum toxin injection into the cricopharyngeus muscle not only confirmed the diagnosis but also provided dramatic, long-lasting relief. Read on to explore this innovative approach.

Authors: Robert Bastian, Melissa Smithson

Retrograde Cricopharyngeal Dysfunction: A Review (2024)

This article explores the inner workings of the upper esophageal sphincter, outlines the challenges of diagnosing RCPD, and reviews current treatment strategies—most notably, botulinum toxin injections. It also delves into how patient communities and social media have been pivotal in raising awareness about this often-overlooked disorder.

Authors: Mattea Miller, Ioan Lina, Lee Akst

Read More

Diagnosis and Management of RCPD: A Systematic Review (2024)

Drawing on data from 17 studies and 637 patients, the article explores how botulinum toxin injections (often at a 50-unit dose) and, when needed, cricopharyngeal myotomy offer effective relief. It also highlights the diagnostic challenges and the emerging role of social media in increasing awareness of this underdiagnosed disorder.

Authors: Raj Malhotra, Hamza Khan, Sydney Zaransky, Joseph Celidonio, Kenneth Yan, Rachel Kaye

Abelchia: inability to belch/burp—a new disorder? RCPD (2021)

This article examines the challenges in diagnosing R‑CPD and highlights how targeted botulinum toxin injections offer lasting relief, significantly improving quality of life.
